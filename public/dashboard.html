Bermuda Gaming & Crown and Anchor

Official requirements to operate a Crown & Anchor table in Bermuda, plus full coverage of Bermuda gaming law, the Bermuda Gaming Commission, and other regulated gaming activities.

⚠️ Legal Notice: This information is provided for general guidance only. Gaming regulations in Bermuda are subject to change. Always verify current requirements directly with the Bermuda Gaming Commission (bgc.bm) and seek independent legal advice before operating any gaming activity.
🏛️ Bermuda Gaming Commission — Official Resources

Crown & Anchor permit applications, legislation, FAQs and all regulatory guidance.

Apply for Permit →

🎲 Crown & Anchor Table — Requirements to Operate

  • ✅
    Eligibility — Who Can Apply You must hold an approved Bermuda liquor licence. Only licensed premises (restaurants, bars, clubs) with a valid liquor licence are eligible to operate Crown & Anchor. You cannot apply without one.
    Liquor Licence Act 1974
  • 📋
    The Permit Required A Crown & Anchor Permit is required in addition to your liquor licence. This is a separate gaming permit issued by the Bermuda Gaming Commission (BGC). You cannot operate without it.
    Liquor Licence Act 1974 s.1(iv)BGC Issued
  • 💰
    Permit Application Fee Application fee: $1,750.00 per permit. This must be paid in full before processing. No partial payments accepted.
    BGC Schedule of Fees
  • 📝
    How to Apply Submit the Crown & Anchor Permit Application Form to the Bermuda Gaming Commission. The form is available at bgc.bm/crown-anchor-application. Once approved, BGC will notify the applicant and issue the permit.
    BGC Application Portal
  • 📊
    Number of Permits Issued The BGC issues approximately 5 Crown & Anchor permits per year. Supply is extremely limited — early application is strongly advised.
    Limited Availability
  • 🔒
    False Information — Criminal Offence Under Section 1(iv) of the Liquor Licence Act 1974: any person who knowingly furnishes false information in any application or required statement commits a criminal offence. All information must be accurate and complete.
    Criminal Offences.1(iv) LLA 1974
  • 🌐
    Public Disclosure All information submitted to the Bermuda Gaming Commission may be subject to public disclosure under the Public Access to Information Act 2010 (PATI). Applicants should be aware that application details may become publicly accessible.
    PATI Act 2010
  • 🏛️
    Regulatory Oversight Crown & Anchor is regulated by the Bermuda Gaming Commission (BGC) since the Gaming (Transfer of Functions) Act. BGC oversees all gaming activities in Bermuda including Crown & Anchor, cruise ship casinos, lotteries, raffles, and betting shops.
    Gaming (Transfer of Functions) Act

📌 Step-by-Step: Crown & Anchor Permit Process

  • 1️⃣
    Step 1 — Confirm Liquor LicenceVerify your premises holds a valid, current Bermuda liquor licence. Check with the Liquor Licensing Authority (lla.gov.bm) if in doubt.
  • 2️⃣
    Step 2 — Download Application FormVisit bgc.bm/crown-anchor-application to download or complete the Crown & Anchor Permit Application Form.
  • 3️⃣
    Step 3 — Complete All Sections AccuratelyFill in all sections truthfully. False information is a criminal offence. Include all required supporting documentation.
  • 4️⃣
    Step 4 — Pay the Application FeePay $1,750.00 in full. Confirm payment method accepted by BGC before submission.
  • 5️⃣
    Step 5 — Submit to BGCSubmit completed application and fee payment to the Bermuda Gaming Commission. Contact BGC directly at bgc.bm for submission details and current processing times.
  • 6️⃣
    Step 6 — Await BGC DecisionBGC will review your application. If approved, you will be notified and issued your Crown & Anchor Permit. With only ~5 permits issued annually, approval is not guaranteed.
  • 7️⃣
    Step 7 — Operate Within Permit ConditionsOperate the Crown & Anchor table strictly within the conditions of your permit. Keep your liquor licence and gaming permit current and accessible on premises.

🎰 Casino Gaming

  • ⚖️
    Governing LawCasino Gaming Act 2014. Established the Bermuda Gaming Commission (originally Bermuda Casino Gaming Commission).
  • 🏨
    Licence LimitMaximum of 3 casino licences in Bermuda. Casinos must be operated as part of an integrated resort (hotel/resort only). No standalone casinos permitted.
  • 📋
    Application ProcessApply for casino licence through BGC (bgc.bm/casinos). Extensive fit-and-proper investigation, financial requirements, and site designation required.
  • 📜
    Key RegulationsGaming (Casino Licence Application) Regs 2017 • Gaming (Casino Fees) Regs 2017 • Gaming (General Reserve & Casino Taxes) Regs 2017 • Gaming (Designated Sites) Regs 2016

🎯 Betting Shops

  • ⚖️
    Governing LawBetting Act 2021. This replaced older betting legislation and introduced specific requirements for the operation of betting shops.
  • 📋
    Licence RequiredSeparate betting operator licence required. Fees governed by Betting Fees Regulations 2021.
  • 👤
    Patron RecordsOperators must regularly update patron details to maintain accurate records and regulatory compliance.
  • 🏛️
    RegulatorBermuda Gaming Commission (BGC). Oversight transferred from previous authority by Gaming (Transfer of Functions) Act.

🚢 Cruise Ship Casinos

  • ⚖️
    Governing LawCruise Ships (Casinos) Act 2013.
  • 📋
    Scale of OperationBGC processes approximately 40–50 cruise ship casino permits per year. Most active gaming licensing category.
  • 🏛️
    RegulatorBermuda Gaming Commission (BGC).

🎟️ Lotteries & Raffles

  • 🏛️
    RegulatorBermuda Gaming Commission (BGC). Oversight included in BGC remit expansion in 2021.
  • 📋
    Permit RequiredLotteries and raffles are regulated activities. A permit must be obtained from BGC before conducting any lottery or raffle.

🌐 Online Gambling

  • 🚫
    Status in BermudaNo online gambling licences have been issued in Bermuda. There are no legally operated online gambling, mobile betting, or e-sports betting sites based in Bermuda.
  • ⚠️
    WarningOperating online gambling without a licence is illegal. Bermudians accessing offshore gambling sites do so under those sites' own legal frameworks — not Bermuda law.
